BISOPROLOL

Indications Mechanism of Action Dosage Forms & Strengths Normal Doses Storage Pharmacokinetics Pregnancy & Lactation Adverse Effects Contraindications Drug Interactions Advice to Patient Brands References

Indications

Bisoprolol is a beta-blocker that affects the heart and circulation. It is used to treat:

  • Hypertension
  • Angina

Mechanism of Action

  • Bisoprolol blocks β-1 receptors (mainly found in the heart), thus slowing the heartbeat.
  • Since the heart is pumping gently, it pushes less blood per beat (drop in blood pressure).
  • This, in turn, reduces both oxygen and energy demand of the heart.

Dosage Forms and Strengths

Tablets containing bisoprolol fumarate in 2.5mg, 5mg, and 10mg are available.

Normal Doses

Adults: 2.5 - 5mg, may increase to 10mg, but maximum 20mg daily.

Children: Not recommended for anyone younger than 18 years (safety and efficacy not established).

Take it as prescribed by the physician.

Storage

Store at room temperature and keep away from moisture and heat.

Pharmacokinetics Profiling

  • Onset of action: 1 - 2 hours
  • Absorption: Rapid and almost complete
  • Metabolism: Hepatic (almost 20% undergo first pass effect)
  • Bioavailability: Approx. 80%
  • Elimination half-life:
    • Normal renal function: 9 - 12 hours
    • CrCl < 40ml/min: 27 - 36 hours
    • Hepatic cirrhosis: 8 - 22 hours
  • Time to peak: 2 - 4 hours
  • Excretion: Urine (>50%), Feces (<2%)

Pregnancy Category and Lactation

Pregnancy Category: C

Excretion in milk unknown, use cautiously.

Adverse Effects

  • Cardiovascular: Bradycardia, postural hypotension, arrhythmias (rarely), AV block
  • Respiratory: Dyspnea, bronchospasm
  • Skin: Dry eyes, rash
  • Cold extremities
  • Arthralgia
  • Myopathy
  • Peripheral neuropathy

Contraindications

  • AV Block (without pacemaker)
  • Severe bradycardia
  • Uncontrolled heart failure
  • Severe asthma
  • Cardiogenic shock
  • Metabolic acidosis
  • Severe pheochromocytoma

Drug Interactions

Drug-Drug Interactions:

  • Calcium channel blockers (Amlodipine, Verapamil, etc.)
  • Antiarrhythmics
  • Antipsychotics
  • Tricyclic antidepressants
  • Cimetidine
  • Insulin and oral hypoglycemics
  • Barbiturates
  • Fluvoxamine

Drug-Food Interactions:

  • Alcohol: Both combined can lower blood pressure.
  • Multivitamins: Decrease the effects of bisoprolol. Administration time should be separated by 2 hours.

Advice to Patient

  • Do NOT take with antacids.
  • Check blood pressure and pulse regularly.
  • In case of diabetes, check blood glucose levels regularly.
  • Contact prescriber if there are unrelieved symptoms or adverse effects.
